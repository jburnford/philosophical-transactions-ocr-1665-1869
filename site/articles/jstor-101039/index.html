In short, he deduces the increase of the Bones through their several intervals of Times, and gives an accompt, How they are constituted in the first two Moneths, in which there hardly appeareth any thing of Bones distinguishable; then what of them, about the Third, fourth, and the rest of the Moneths, is ordinarily changed, added, or taken away, at least in the Fætus of these Climats. The sum is, That there is a Transmigration of Membranous parts into Cartilaginous, and from them into Bony ones; performed by Nature with such silent steps, that the most quick-sighted, and the most patient Eye shall never see it doing, though it may evidently see it done.
