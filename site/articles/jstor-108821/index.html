III. On the Contact of Curves. By William Spottiswoode, M.A., F.R.S. Received October 15,—Read November 21, 1861. Let \( U = 0 \) be the equation to the curve with which the curve \( V = 0 \) has an "m-pointic contact;" in other words, let the curves \( U \) and \( V \) have \( m \) consecutive points in common. The degree of contact of which \( V \) is capable is equal to the number of independent constants contained in its equation; i.e. to the number of terms in its complete expression, less one. Thus if \( n \) be the degree of \( V \), the degree of contact will be \( \frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{1.2} - 1 \). If the curves \( U \) and \( V \) have only a single point in common, then the only conditions are \[ U = 0, \quad V = 0. \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (1.) \] If they have two consecutive points in common, then beside (1.) we have also \[ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} dx + \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} dy + \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} dz &= 0, \\ \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} dx + \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} dy + \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} dz &= 0, \end{align*} \] which, as is well known, lead to the conditions \[ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} : \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} : \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \\ = \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} : \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} : \frac{\partial V}{\partial z}; \end{align*} \] and if \( V \) be linear, \( = lx + my + nz \), (3.) will suffice to determine the ratios \( l:m:n \), and fix the position of the tangent \( V \). The conditions (3.) may be expressed by a single equation thus: if \( \alpha, \beta, \gamma \) be any arbitrary quantities, then the equation \[ \begin{vmatrix} \alpha & \beta & \gamma \\ \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \\ \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} \end{vmatrix} = 0, \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (4.) \] considered as identical in \( \alpha, \beta, \gamma \), may be regarded as an expression for the required conditions. The developed form of (4.) is \[ \left( \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right) \frac{dV}{dx} + \left( \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} - \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \right) \frac{dV}{dy} + \left( \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right) \frac{dV}{dz} = 0; \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (5.) \] so that, writing \[ \left( \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right) \frac{\partial}{\partial x} + \left( \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} - \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \right) \frac{\partial}{\partial y} + \left( \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right) \frac{\partial}{\partial z} = \square, \quad (6.) \] the effect of the operation \( \square \) upon \( V \) is equivalent to the elimination of \( dx, dy, dz \), from (1.) and their differentials (2.), and is expressed by the equation \[ \square V = 0. \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (7.) \] If the curves have three consecutive points in common, we have, in addition to former conditions, \[ \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} d^3x + \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} d^3y + \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} d^3z + \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x^2} dx^2 + \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial y^2} dy^2 + \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial z^2} dz^2 + 2 \left( \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial y \partial z} dydz + \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial z \partial x} dzdx + \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x \partial y} dxdy \right) = 0, \] \[ \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} d^3x + \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} d^3y + \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} d^3z + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial x^2} dx^2 + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial y^2} dy^2 + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial z^2} dz^2 + 2 \left( \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial y \partial z} dydz + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial z \partial x} dzdx + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial x \partial y} dxdy \right) = 0, \] from which \( dx, dy, dz, d^2x, d^2y, d^2z \) are to be eliminated by the help of (1.) and (2.), or (3.). Instead, however, of differentiating (2.), we may differentiate (3.), or, what is the same thing, (4.); and we shall then have an expression free from \( d^2x, d^2y, d^2z \). The results of the elimination may, moreover, in this case be grouped into a single expression. Writing for convenience \[ \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial}{\partial z} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial}{\partial y} = D_1, \quad \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial}{\partial z} = D_2, \quad \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} = D_3, \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (9.) \] (3.) may be expressed by any two of the equations \[ D_1 V = 0, \quad D_2 V = 0, \quad D_3 V = 0. \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (10.) \] Differentiating these and combining their differentials with the first of (2.), we may form the quadratic system, \[ \begin{align*} D_1^2 V &= 0 \\ D_1 D_2 V &= 0 \\ D_1 D_3 V &= 0 \\ D_2 D_1 V &= 0 \\ D_2^2 V &= 0 \\ D_2 D_3 V &= 0 \\ D_3 D_1 V &= 0 \\ D_3 D_2 V &= 0 \\ D_3^2 V &= 0, \end{align*} \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (11.) \] all of which are comprised in the one equation \[ \square^2 V = 0 \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (12.) \] And generally, by a similar train of reasoning, the constants of a curve \( V \), having an \( m \)-pointic contact with \( U \), will be determined by the equations \[ V = 0, \quad \square V = 0, \quad . . \square^m V = 0; \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (13.) \] and if \[ V = (a, b, . . k \langle x, y, z \rangle^n) \] \[ = a \frac{\partial V}{\partial a} + b \frac{\partial V}{\partial b} + . . k \frac{\partial V}{\partial k}, \quad . . . . . . . . . . . . . . (14.) \] then \[ a : b : \ldots k = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial V}{\partial a} & \frac{\partial V}{\partial b} & \ldots & \frac{\partial V}{\partial k} \\ \square \frac{\partial V}{\partial a} & \square \frac{\partial V}{\partial b} & \ldots & \square \frac{\partial V}{\partial k} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \square^{m-1} \frac{\partial V}{\partial a} & \square^{m-1} \frac{\partial V}{\partial b} & \ldots & \square^{m-1} \frac{\partial V}{\partial k} \end{vmatrix} \ldots \ldots \ldots (15.) \] The equations (11.) are, however, not all independent. In the first place, as in (10.) any one of the three equations is a consequence of the other two, so in (12.) any one column or row is a consequence of the other two. Secondly, if any pair of binary combinations of the Ds, e.g. \(D_1D_2V\) and \(D_2D_3V\), be developed, they will, with the help of (10.) or (3.), be found identical. This reduces the system (12.) to three independent conditions, of which \[ D^2_1V = 0, \quad D_1D_2V = 0, \quad D^2_2V = 0 \ldots \ldots \ldots (16.) \] is a type; as it should do. These three equations will suffice to determine the three independent constants of \(V\), when it is capable of a 3-pointic contact, and no more, with \(U\). The actual calculations may be considerably simplified by using, instead of (13.), the following system, \[ V = 0, \quad DV = 0, \quad D^2V = 0 \] (where, as before, \(D\) stands for any one of the symbols \(D_1, D_2, D_3\)). Of this system (16.) is a consequence. By way of example, we have for the ordinary tangent \[ V = ax + by + cz = 0, \] \[ DV = aDx + bDy + cDz = 0, \] whence \[ a : b : c = \begin{vmatrix} x & y & z \\ Dx & Dy & Dz \end{vmatrix} \ldots \ldots \ldots (17.) \] \[ = yDz - zDy : zDx - xDz : xDy - yDx \] \[ = y \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} + z \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} : -x \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} : -x \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \] \[ = \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} : \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} : \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}. \ldots \ldots \ldots (18.) \] The equation of the circle of curvature may be put in the form \[ hr^2 + 2(ayz + bxz + cxy) = 0; \] to which are to be added, \[ hD^2r^2 + 2(aDyz + bDzx + cDxy) = 0, \] \[ hD^2r^2 + 2(aD^2yz + bD^2zx + cD^2xy) = 0, \] whence \[ h : a : b : c \] \[ \begin{vmatrix} r^2 & 2yz & 2zx & 2xy \\ Dr^2 & 2Dyz & 2Dzx & 2Dxy \\ D^2r^2 & 2D^2yz & 2D^2zx & 2D^2xy \end{vmatrix} \quad \ldots \ldots \ldots (19.) \] also \[ D^2r^2 = 2 \left( z \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - y \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right), \] \[ D^2yz = 2 \left( \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right)^2 \right) + 2 \left( z \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - y \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right), \] \[ Dyz = y \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - z \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ D^2yz = -2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} + y \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - z \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ Dzx = x \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \quad D^2zx = x \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \] \[ Dxy = -x \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \quad D^2xy = -x \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}. \] Moreover the minors formed from the first two rows of the matrix, and from the columns below written, have the following values: \[ r^2, 2yz = x \left\{ -(y^2 - z^2) \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} + xy \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - xz \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right\}, \] \[ r^2, 2zx = x \left\{ -yx \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - (z^2 - x^2) \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} + yz \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right\}, \] \[ r^2, 2xy = x \left\{ zx \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - zy \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - (x^2 - y^2) \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right\}, \] \[ 2zx, 2xy = 2xz \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \] \[ 2xy, 2yz = 2xy \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \] \[ 2yz, 2zx = 2xz \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}; \] whence, finally, \[ h : a : b : c \] \[ = 4 \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} + \frac{2xyz}{(n-1)^2} H \] \[ : 2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \left\{ \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right)^2 \right\} + \left( x^2 - y^2 - z^2 \right) \frac{x}{(n-1)^2} H \] \[ : 2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \left\{ \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \right)^2 \right\} + \left( -x^2 + y^2 - z^2 \right) \frac{y}{(n-1)^2} H \] \[ : 2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \left\{ \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right)^2 \right\} + \left( -x^2 - y^2 + z^2 \right) \frac{z}{(n-1)^2} H, \ldots \ldots (20.) \] where \( H \) is the Hessian of \( U \). Proceeding by the same principles, we shall have merely to replace $\square$ by $D$ in (15.), and there will result a system of expressions for the constants of the curve $V$ having an $m$-pointic contact with a given curve $U$. The following method of reduction may sometimes be used with advantage. Let $\xi$, $\eta$, $\zeta$, $\ldots \phi$, $\chi$, $\psi$ stand for the powers and products of the variables forming the various terms of $V$. Then the coefficients of the curve $V$ having an $m$-pointic contact with $U$ will be proportional to the determinants of which the following is a type: $$\begin{vmatrix} D^{m-1}\xi & D^{m-1}\eta & \ldots & D^{m-1}\psi \\ D^{m-2}\xi & D^{m-2}\eta & \ldots & D^{m-2}\psi \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \xi & \eta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix}$$ But this $$= -\sum D^{m-2}\xi \begin{vmatrix} D^{m-1}\eta & D^{m-1}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-1}\psi \\ D^{m-3}\eta & D^{m-3}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-3}\psi \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \eta & \zeta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix} = -\sum D^{m-2}\xi \begin{vmatrix} D^{m-2}\eta & D^{m-2}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-2}\psi \\ D^{m-3}\eta & D^{m-3}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-3}\psi \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \eta & \zeta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix}$$ $$=(-)^2 \sum D^{m-2}\xi \begin{vmatrix} D^{m-3}\eta & D^{m-3}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-3}\psi \\ D^{m-4}\eta & D^{m-4}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-4}\psi \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \eta & \zeta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix}$$ $$=(-)^2 \sum D^{m-2}\xi \begin{vmatrix} D^{m-3}\eta & D^{m-3}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-3}\psi \\ D^{m-4}\eta & D^{m-4}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-4}\psi \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \eta & \zeta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix}$$ $$=(-)^{m-2} \sum D^{m-2}\xi \sum D^{m-3}\eta \begin{vmatrix} D^{m-3}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-3}\psi \\ D^{m-4}\zeta & \ldots & D^{m-4}\psi \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \zeta & \ldots & \psi. \end{vmatrix}$$ $$=\begin{vmatrix} -\sum D^3x^2 & \sum D^2y^2 & \sum Dz^2D^3 & 2Dzx & 2Dxy \\ zx & xy \end{vmatrix}.$$ In the case of the conic of 5-pointic contact this becomes $$-\sum D^3x^2 \sum D^2y^2 \sum Dz^2D^3 | 2Dzx 2Dxy |$$ I now proceed to the calculation of the conic of 5-pointic contact. This may be effected by any of the three methods indicated above, viz. (1) by means of the symbol $\square$, getting out the factors $(ax + \beta y + \gamma z)^p$; (2) by means of the symbols $D$; (3) by the reduced formula (21.). As most of the steps have been calculated by two methods, I subjoin some of the leading formulæ and transformations which occur in the process. Writing for convenience \[ \lambda = \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \quad \mu = \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} - \gamma \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \quad \nu = \beta \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - \alpha \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \] and putting \[ V = (abcfg \chi xyz)^2, \] the general expressions (13.) become \[ 0 = (abcfg \chi \chi xyz)^2, \] \[ 0 = (abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \chi xyz), \] \[ 0 = (abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz) + (abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz), \] \[ 0 = 3(abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz) + (abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz), \] \[ 0 = 3(abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz) + 4(abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz) + (abcfg \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi \lambda \mu \nu \chi xyz), \] whence \[ a : b : c : f : g : h = \] \[ \begin{array}{cccccc} 3(\Box \lambda)^2 + 4\lambda \Box^2 \lambda + x \Box^3 \lambda & . & 6 \Box \mu \Box \nu + 4(\mu \Box^2 \nu + \nu \Box^2 \mu) + y \Box^3 \nu + z \Box^3 \mu & . \\ 3\lambda \Box \lambda + x \Box^2 \lambda & . & 3(\mu \Box \nu + \nu \Box \mu) + y \Box^2 \nu + z \Box^2 \mu & . \\ \lambda^2 + x \Box \lambda & . & 2\mu \nu + y \Box \nu + z \Box \mu & . \\ x\lambda & . & y\mu + z\nu & . \\ x^2 & y^2 & z^2 & 2yz & 2zx & 2xy. \end{array} \] The most convenient method for developing these formulæ is to begin with forming the 15 minors derived from the last two lines; and by means of them, the 20 minors derived from the last three lines; and so on. It may be noticed that in order to pass to the corresponding expressions in D, e.g. D₁, it is only necessary to make \( \beta = 0, \gamma = 0, \) and to divide out the \( \alpha s. \) This gives \[ \lambda = 0, \quad \mu = \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \quad \nu = -\frac{\partial U}{\partial y}. \] The results of the calculations are as follow: the third minors, formed from the two lower lines of (22.), and from the columns the lower constituents of which alone are written down, will be \[ y^2, z^2 = -yz \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \quad z^2, x^2 = -zx \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \quad x^2, y^2 = -xy \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ 2zx, 2xy = 2x^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \quad 2xy, 2yz = 2y^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \quad 2yz, 2zx = 2z^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ x^2, 2yz = x \left( y \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} - z \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right), \quad x^2, 2zx = x^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \quad x^2, 2xy = -x^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ y^2, 2yz = -y^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \quad y^2, 2zx = y \left( z \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} - x \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \right), \quad y^2, 2xy = y^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial z}, \] \[ z^2, 2yz = z^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial x}, \quad z^2, 2zx = -z^2 \frac{\partial U}{\partial y}, \quad z^2, 2xy = z \left( x \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} - y \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \right). \] Similarly, for the second minors, \[ x^2, 2xy, 2xz = x^3(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2xy, y^2, 2yz = y^3(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2xz, 2yz, z^2 = z^3(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ x^2, 2yz, 2zx = 2\left(\frac{\partial U}{dy}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dz} - zx^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ x^2, 2xy, 2yz = 2\frac{\partial U}{dy}\left(\frac{\partial U}{dz}\right)^2 - yx^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2xy, y^2, 2zx = 2\left(\frac{\partial U}{dz}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dx} - xy^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2xz, y^2, 2yz = 2\frac{\partial U}{dz}\left(\frac{\partial U}{dx}\right)^2 - zy^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2xy, 2yz, z^2 = 2\left(\frac{\partial U}{dx}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dy} - yz^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2zx, 2xy, z^2 = 2\frac{\partial U}{dx}\left(\frac{\partial U}{dy}\right)^2 - xz^2(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ y^3, z^3, 2yz = \left(\frac{\partial U}{dx}\right)^3, \] \[ y^3, z^3, 2zx = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dx}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dy} + yz^2(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ y^3, z^3, 2xy = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dx}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dz} + y^2z(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ z^3, x^3, 2yz = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dy}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dx} + z^2x(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ z^3, x^3, 2zx = \left(\frac{\partial U}{dy}\right)^3, \] \[ z^3, x^3, 2xy = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dy}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dz} + zx^2(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ x^3, y^2, 2yz = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dz}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dx} + xy^2(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ x^3, y^2, 2zx = -\left\{\left(\frac{\partial U}{dz}\right)^2\frac{\partial U}{dy} + x^2y(n-1)^{-2}H\right\}, \] \[ x^3, y^2, 2xy = \left(\frac{\partial U}{dz}\right)^3, \] \[ x^3, y^2, z^2 = \frac{\partial U}{dx}\frac{\partial U}{dy}\frac{\partial U}{dz} - xyz(n-1)^{-2}H, \] \[ 2yz, 2zx, 2xy = -2\frac{\partial U}{dx}\frac{\partial U}{dy}\frac{\partial U}{dz} - 2xyz(n-1)^{-2}H. \] Similarly, for the first minors, \[ x^2, y^2, z^2, 2yz = \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial H}{\partial z} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial H}{\partial y} \right) - 3H \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial z^2} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial y^2} \right), \] \[ x^2, y^2, z^2, 2zx = \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial H}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial H}{\partial z} \right) - 3H \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x^2} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial z^2} \right), \] \[ z^2, x^2, 2xy, 2yz = \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial H}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial H}{\partial z} \right) - 3H \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x^2} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial x} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial z^2} \right) + 3H \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial y} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x^2} -
