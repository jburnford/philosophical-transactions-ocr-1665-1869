He wanders from Town to Town to see who has any thing to cypher, and so gets some money; but would fain learn to read and write. This I mention because it is so prodigious, and he divides almost with as much ease as he multiplies, and reduces things to the least denomination in fractions. I have a great mind, could I be assured of his fidelity, to take him into my house, and learn him to read, write and cypher. Errata in the Philosophical Transactions.
