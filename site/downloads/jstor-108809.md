XXIV. On the Calculus of Symbols.—Third Memoir. By W. H. L. Russell, Esq., A.B.

Communicated by A. Cayley, F.R.S.

Received May 15,—Read May 21, 1863.

In my second Memoir "On the Calculus of Symbols," I worked out the general case of multiplication according to one of the two systems of combination of non-commutative symbols previously given. In the present paper I propose to investigate the general case of multiplication according to the other system. I commence with the Binomial Theorem, to which the second system gives rise. In my previous researches I obtained the general term of the binomial theorem when the symbols combine according to the first system by equating symbolical coefficients; here, on the other hand, I consider the nature of the combinations which arise from the symbolical multiplication, and obtain the general term by summation. I next proceed to the multiplication of binomial factors. Here the general term is obtained by considering the alteration of weight undergone by certain symbols in the process of multiplication. The multinomial theorem according to the second system is next considered and its general term calculated. I conclude the memoir with some applications of the calculus of symbols to successive differentiation. This paper completes the investigation of symbolical multiplication and division according to the two systems of combination, the general case of division having been worked out by Mr. Spottiswoode in a very beautiful memoir recently published in the Transactions of this Society.

I shall commence with expanding the binomial \((\pi + \theta \xi)^n\) in terms of \(\pi\).

I shall put \(\delta\) in place of the symbol \(\xi \frac{d}{d\xi}\), and write \(a_a\), for \(a, \frac{a-1}{2}, \frac{a-2}{3}, \ldots, \frac{a-a+1}{a_1}\).

Now

\[
(\pi + \theta \xi)^2 = \pi^2 + \theta \xi \cdot \pi + \theta \xi^2 + \theta (\xi)^2,
\]

\[
(\pi + \theta \xi)^3 = \pi^3 + (\pi^2 \theta \xi + \pi \theta \xi^2 + \theta \xi^3) + (\theta \xi^2 \pi + \theta \xi \theta \xi + \pi \theta \xi^3) + \theta \xi^3.
\]

Hence we evidently have

\[
(\pi + \theta \xi)^n = \pi^n + \Sigma(\pi^{n-1} \theta \xi) + \Sigma(\pi^{n-2} \theta \xi^2) + \Sigma(\pi^{n-3} \theta \xi^3) + \ldots,
\]

where \(\Sigma(\pi^m \theta \xi^r)\) denotes the sum of all the factors in which the symbol \((\pi)\) occurs \((m)\) times and the symbol \((\theta \xi)\) \((r)\) times irrespective of position.

Now if \(a + b = m\),

\[
\pi^b \theta \xi^a = \theta \xi^a \pi^b + b_1 \delta \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-1} + b_2 \delta^2 \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-2} + b_3 \delta^3 \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-3} + \ldots
\]

Again, if \(a + b + c = m\),

\[
\pi^c \theta \xi^b \theta \xi^a = \theta \xi^a \pi^c + b_1 \delta \theta \xi^b \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-1} + b_2 \delta^2 \theta \xi^b \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-2} + b_3 \delta^3 \theta \xi^b \theta \xi^a \pi^{m-3} + \ldots

Now the general term of the binomial \((\pi + \theta^e)^n\) will be the term involving \(\pi^a\) in the sum of

\[
\Sigma (\pi^{n-1} \theta^e), \quad \Sigma (\pi^{n-2} \theta^e^2), \quad \&c.
\]
Hence we easily see that the coefficient of $\pi^n$ in the binomial is

$$\sum_{n-1} \sum_{n-\mu-1} b_0 \delta^{\alpha} \theta^{\beta} + \sum_{n-2} \sum_{n-\mu-2} b_0 c_0 \delta^{\alpha} \theta^{\beta} \theta^{\gamma} + \sum_{n-3} \sum_{n-\mu-3} b_0 c_0 e_0 \delta^{\alpha} \theta^{\beta} \theta^{\gamma} \theta^{\delta} + \ldots$$

$$+ \sum_{n-\nu} \sum_{n-\mu-\nu} b_0 c_0 \ldots l_0 \delta^{\alpha} \theta^{\beta} \ldots \delta^{\alpha} \theta^{\beta} \theta^{\gamma} + \ldots,$$

where

$$a + b + c + e + \ldots + l = n - \nu$$

$$b_0 + c_0 + \ldots + l_0 = n - \mu - \nu.$$

I shall next investigate the general term of the symbolical product,

$$(\pi + \theta_1)(\pi + \theta_2)(\pi + \theta_3) \ldots (\pi + \theta_n).$$

Now

$$(\pi + \theta_1)(\pi + \theta_2) = \pi^2 + \pi \theta_1 + \theta_2 \pi + \theta_1 \theta_2;$$

$$(\pi + \theta_1)(\pi + \theta_2)(\pi + \theta_3) = \pi^3 + (\theta_1 \pi^2 + \pi \theta_2 \pi + \pi^2 \theta_3)$$

$$+ (\theta_1 \theta_2 \pi + \theta_1 \theta_3 \pi + \theta_2 \theta_3 \pi) + \theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_3;$$

$$(\pi + \theta_1)(\pi + \theta_2)(\pi + \theta_3)(\pi + \theta_4) = \pi^4 + (\theta_1 \pi^3 + \pi \theta_2 \pi^2 + \pi^2 \theta_3 \pi + \pi^3 \theta_4)$$

$$+ (\theta_1 \theta_2 \pi^2 + \theta_1 \theta_3 \pi^2 + \theta_1 \theta_4 \pi^2 + \theta_2 \theta_3 \pi^2 + \theta_2 \theta_4 \pi^2 + \theta_3 \theta_4 \pi^2)$$

$$+ (\theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_3 \pi + \theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_4 \pi + \theta_1 \theta_3 \theta_4 \pi + \theta_2 \theta_3 \theta_4 \pi) + \theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_3 \theta_4.$$ 

Hence we deduce the following law of multiplication in this case.

$$\pi^a \theta_{a_1}(\varepsilon) \pi^b \theta_{b_2}(\varepsilon) \pi^c \theta_{c_3}(\varepsilon) \ldots \pi^r \theta_{r_r}(\varepsilon) \pi^s,$$

where

$$a + b + c + \ldots + s = n - r, \ldots \ldots \ldots \ldots (1.)$$

$$a_1 = a + 1,$$

$$b_2 = a + b + 2,$$

$$c_3 = a + b + c + 3,$$

&c. = &c.,

$$l_r = a + b + c + \ldots + l + r,$$

and let $a, b, c, \ldots s$ have all the values which can be assigned consistently with equation (1.); the sum of the symbolical terms thus formed will be that group of the symbolical product in which the factor $\pi$ occurs $(n-r)$ times. And by giving $(r)$ the successive values $0, 1, 2, 3 \ldots n$, we obtain the entire product, and may write

$$(\pi + \theta_1)(\pi + \theta_2)(\pi + \theta_3) \ldots (\pi + \theta_n) = \pi^n + \sum \theta_1 \pi^{n-1} + \sum \theta_1 \theta_2 \pi^{n-2} + \sum \theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_3 \pi^{n-3} + \ldots$$

$$+ \sum \theta_1 \theta_2 \theta_3 \theta_4 \ldots \theta_r \pi^{n-r} + \ldots,$$

where $\sum \theta_1 \pi^{n-1}$, &c. are the groups we have just considered.
Now if $\psi$, $\phi$, $\theta$ are any functions of $(\xi)$, and $a + b + c + e = m$, we have

$$\pi^a \psi^c \phi^b \theta^n = \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-1} + b_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots + c_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-1} + b_1 c_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots + c_2 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots + e_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-1} + b_1 e_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots + c_1 e_1 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots + e_2 \psi \phi \theta \pi^{m-2} + \ldots$$

where $b_1$, $c_1$, &c. have their original significations.

Hence we see that the general term of $\pi^a \theta_1 \xi \ldots \pi^a \theta_r (\xi) \pi^a \theta_{r-1} \xi \pi^a \theta_r \xi \pi^a$ is

$$\sum b_0 c_0 \ldots l_0 \delta^b_0 \theta_1 \xi \ldots \delta^b_{r-1} \theta_{r-1} (\xi) \delta^b_r \theta_r (\xi) \pi^{m-s},$$

$$a + b + c + e + \ldots + l = m,$$

$$b_0 + c_0 + e_0 + \ldots + l_0 = s;$$

hence the general term of $\sum \theta_1 \xi \theta_2 \xi \ldots \theta_r \xi \pi^m$ is

$$\sum \sum b_0 c_0 \ldots l_0 \delta^b_0 \theta_1 \xi \ldots \delta^b_{r-1} \theta_{r-1} (\xi) \delta^b_r \theta_r (\xi) \pi^{m-s};$$

and we find the symbolical coefficient of $\pi^m$ in $(\pi + \theta_1 \xi)(\pi + \theta_2 \xi)(\pi + \theta_3 \xi) \ldots (\pi + \theta_r \xi)$ to be

$$\sum \sum \sum \sum \sum b_0 c_0 \ldots l_0 \delta^b_0 \theta_1 \xi \ldots \delta^b_{r-1} \theta_{r-1} (\xi) \delta^b_r \theta_r (\xi) \pi^{m-s} + &c.$$ 

where

$$a + b + c + \ldots + l = n - r,$$

$$b_0 + c_0 + \ldots + l_0 = n - \mu - \nu.$$

Let us now expand the binomial $(\pi^a + \theta(\xi) \pi)^n$. We might of course do this by putting

$$\theta_1 \xi = \theta_2 \xi = \ldots = \theta_r \xi = 0$$

in the previous investigation, but we shall proceed as follows:

$$(\pi^a + \theta(\xi) \pi)^n = \pi^{2m} + \sum (\pi^a)^{n-1} (\theta(\xi) \pi) + \sum (\pi^a)^{n-2} (\theta(\xi) \pi)^2 + &c. + \sum (\pi^a)^{n-r} (\theta(\xi) \pi)^r + \ldots,$$

and the general term of $\sum (\pi^a)^m (\theta(\xi) \pi)^r$ will be

$$\sum \sum (2b+1)_0 (2c+1)_0 \ldots (2l)_0 \delta^b_0 \theta_1 \xi \ldots \delta^b_{r-1} \theta_{r-1} (\xi) \delta^b_r \theta_r (\xi) \pi^{2m+r-s},$$

where

$$a + b + c + \ldots + l = m,$$

$$b_0 + c_0 + \ldots + l_0 = s,$$

and the symbolical coefficient of $\pi^m$ in the binomial will be

$$\sum \sum \sum \sum \sum (2b+1)_0 (2c+1)_0 \ldots (2l)_0 \delta^b_0 \theta_1 \xi \ldots \delta^b_{r-1} \theta_{r-1} (\xi) \delta^b_r \theta_r (\xi) + \ldots$$
where

\[ a + b + c + \ldots + l = n - v, \]
\[ b_0 + c_0 + \ldots + l_0 = 2n - \mu - v. \]

To determine the general term in the expansion of the multinomial expression

\[ (\pi^n + \theta_1 \xi \pi^{n-1} + \theta_2 \xi \pi^{n-2} + \theta_3 \xi \pi^{n-3} + \ldots)^m. \]

\[ \pi^{n\alpha} (\theta_1 \xi \pi^{n-1})^{\beta_1} (\theta_2 \xi \pi^{n-2})^{\beta_2} \ldots \pi^{n\alpha} (\theta_1 \xi \pi^{n-1})^{\beta_1} (\theta_2 \xi \pi^{n-2})^{\beta_2} \ldots \pi^{n\alpha} (\theta_1 \xi \pi^{n-1})^{\beta_1} (\theta_2 \xi \pi^{n-2})^{\beta_2} \ldots, \]

where

\[ \alpha_1 + \alpha_2 + \alpha_3 + \ldots = \alpha, \]
\[ \beta_1 + \beta_2 + \beta_3 + \ldots = \beta, \]
\[ \gamma_1 + \gamma_2 + \gamma_3 + \ldots = \gamma, \ldots \]

Now the symbolical coefficient of

\[ nm - (\beta + 2\gamma + 3\xi + \ldots) - s \]

in the preceding expression will be as follows:

\[ \Sigma (n-3)\xi(n-3)\xi'' \ldots (\zeta_3 \text{ factors})(n-2)\xi(n-2)\xi'' \ldots (\gamma_3 \text{ factors})(n-1)\xi(n-1)\xi'' \ldots (\beta_3 \text{ factors})(n\alpha_3)\xi' \ldots \]

\[ (n-3)\xi(n-3)\xi'' \ldots (\zeta_2 \text{ factors})(n-2)\xi(n-2)\xi'' \ldots (\gamma_2 \text{ factors})(n-1)\xi(n-1)\xi'' \ldots (\beta_2 \text{ factors})(n\alpha_2)\xi' \ldots \]

\[ (n-3)\xi(n-3)\xi'' \ldots (\zeta_1 \text{ factors})(n-2)\xi(n-2)\xi'' \ldots (\gamma_1 \text{ factors})(n-1)\xi(n-1)\xi'' \ldots (\beta_1 \text{ factors})(n\alpha_1)\xi' \ldots \]

\[ \delta^{\alpha_1} \theta_1 \xi \delta^{\beta_1} \theta_2 \xi \delta^{\gamma_1} \theta_3 \xi \ldots \delta^{\alpha_2} \theta_1 \xi \delta^{\beta_2} \theta_2 \xi \delta^{\gamma_2} \theta_3 \xi \ldots \delta^{\alpha_3} \theta_1 \xi \delta^{\beta_3} \theta_2 \xi \delta^{\gamma_3} \theta_3 \xi \ldots \ldots \]

where the sum of the indices of \( \delta \) must equal \( s \).

From this expression, by putting

\[ nm - (\beta + 2\gamma + 3\xi + \ldots) - s = \mu, \]

the symbolical coefficient of \( \pi^\alpha \) in the expression of the given multinomial may be immediately deduced.

There are certain methods of expressing the differential coefficients of implicit functions by means of symbolical notation which I notice in this place, as the method of summation employed for that purpose is similar to some of the symbolical summations we have already considered in this paper.
Let $F(x, y)$ be a function of $x$ and $y$, $y$ being a function of $(x)$, and $y_1, y_2, y_3, \ldots$ the successive differential coefficients of $(y)$. Then

$$\frac{dF}{dx} = \frac{dF}{dx} + \frac{dF}{dy} y_1,$$

$$\frac{d^2F}{dx^2} = \frac{d^2F}{dx^2} + 2 \frac{d^2F}{dxdy} y_1 + \frac{d^2F}{dy^2} y_1^2 + \frac{dF}{dy} y_2,$$

$$\frac{d^3F}{dx^3} = \frac{d^3F}{dx^3} + 3 \frac{d^3F}{dx^2dy} y_1 + 3 \frac{d^3F}{dxdy^2} y_1^2 + \frac{d^3F}{dy^3} y_1^3 + 3 \frac{d^2F}{dxdy} y_2 + 3 \frac{d^2F}{dy^2} y_1 y_2 + \frac{dF}{dy} y_3,$$

$$\frac{d^4F}{dx^4} = \frac{d^4F}{dx^4} + 4 \frac{d^4F}{dx^3dy} y_1 + 6 \frac{d^4F}{dx^2dy^2} y_1^2 + 4 \frac{d^4F}{dxdy^3} y_1^3 + \frac{d^4F}{dy^4} y_1^4$$

$$+ 6 y_2 \left( \frac{d^3F}{dx^2dy} + 2 \frac{d^3F}{dxdy^2} y_1 + \frac{d^3F}{dy^3} \right) + 4 \frac{d^2F}{dxdy} y_3 + \frac{d^2F}{dy^2} (4 y_1 y_2 + 3 y_2^2) + \frac{dF}{dy} y_4.$$

Now let $U_{r,m,n}$ be the coefficient of $\frac{dm+n}{dx^m dy^n} F$ in the expansion of $\frac{d^rF}{dx^r}$. Then

$$\frac{d^rF}{dx^r} = \frac{d^rF}{dx^r} + r \frac{d^rF}{dx^{r-1}dy} y_1 + r \cdot \frac{r-1}{2} \frac{d^rF}{dx^{r-2}dy^2} y_2 + r \cdot \frac{r-1}{2} \cdot \frac{r-2}{3} \frac{d^rF}{dx^{r-3}dy^3} y_3$$

$$+ r \cdot \frac{r-1}{2} y_2 \left( \frac{d^{r-1}F}{dx^{r-2}dy} + (r-2) \frac{d^{r-1}F}{dx^{r-3}dy^2} y_1 + \frac{(r-2)(r-3)}{1 \cdot 2} \frac{d^{r-1}F}{dx^{r-4}dy^3} y_1^2 + \text{&c.} \right)$$

$$+ \frac{d^{r-2}F}{dx^{r-3}dy} U_{r-3,1} + \frac{d^{r-2}F}{dx^{r-4}dy^2} U_{r-4,2} + \frac{d^{r-2}F}{dx^{r-5}dy^3} U_{r-5,3} + \ldots$$

$$+ \frac{d^{r-3}F}{dx^{r-4}dy} U_{r-4,1} + \frac{d^{r-3}F}{dx^{r-5}dy^2} U_{r-5,2}$$

$$+ \frac{d^{r-4}F}{dx^{r-5}dy} U_{r-5,1} + \text{&c.}$$

We easily obtain this law of coefficients,

$$U_{r,m,n} = \frac{d}{dx} U_{r-1,m,n} + y_1 U_{r-1,m,n-1} + U_{r-1,m-1,n},$$

whence

$$U_{0,m,n} = \frac{d}{dx} U_{0,m,n} + y_1 U_{0,m,n-1}$$

$$= \frac{d^2}{dx^2} U_{0,m,n} + \left( \frac{d}{dx} y_1 + y_1 \frac{d}{dx} \right) U_{0,m,n-1} + y_1^2 U_{0,m,n-2}$$

$$= \frac{d^3}{dx^3} U_{0,m,n} + \Sigma \left( \frac{d^2}{dx^2} y_1 \right) U_{0,m,n-1} + \Sigma \left( \frac{d}{dx} y_1^2 \right) U_{0,m,n-2} + y_1^3 U_{0,m,n-3}$$

$$= \frac{d^{r-1}}{dx^{r-1}} U_{1,m,n} + \Sigma \left( \frac{d^{r-2}}{dx^{r-2}} y_1 \right) U_{1,m,n-1}$$

$$+ \Sigma \left( \frac{d^{r-3}}{dx^{r-3}} y_1^2 \right) U_{1,m,n-2} + \ldots + \Sigma \left( \frac{d^{r-v-1}}{dx^{r-v-1}} y_1^v \right) U_{1,m,n-v} + \text{&c.}$$
Let \( n - r = 1 \), then \( U_{0,n-r}^i = U_{0,1}^i = y_1 \), and \( U_{0,n-1}^i = U_{0,n-2}^i = \ldots = c = 0 \). Hence

\[
U_{r,n}^i = \Sigma \left( \frac{d^{r-n}}{dx^{r-n}} y_1^{n-1} \right) y,
\]

where \( \Sigma \left( \frac{d^{r-n}}{dx^{r-n}} y_1^{n-1} \right) \) denotes the sum of all the symbolical products in which \( \frac{d}{dx} \) occurs \( r-n \) times, and \( y_1 (n-1) \) times.

We have

\[
U_{r,n}^i = \frac{d}{dx} U_{r-1,n}^i + y_1 U_{r-1,n-1}^i + U_{r-1,n}^i,
\]

from which, substituting for \( U_{r-1,n}^i \) the value just obtained, \( U_{r,n}^i \) may be found in a similar manner, and so we may proceed. I shall not, however, enter upon these higher coefficients, my object being principally to call attention to the use of symbolical expressions in expansions of this nature.

The subject of implicit differentiation has been treated by Mr. George Scott of Trinity College, Dublin, in a very elegant paper in the Quarterly Journal of Mathematics, vol. iv. p. 77. His results have great generality, but do not appear to include the above.
