Instantaneously, a perfect hurricane in the north-west quarter, which brought along with it a prodigious quantity of snow, and also a very remarkable change in the temperature of the air.

* About half past 9 p.m., being just going to sleep, I was disturbed by a noise which I at first mistook for the report of one of the guns (three pounders) which are on the top of the house; but which, I have since been informed by the officers of the factory, was caused by one of the beams of the house being rent by the expansive power of the frost. W. W.

† Heard a very uncommon sound, seeming to be within the factory; and near as I can liken it, it was the same as firing a small carriage gun; the factory people say it is very frequent in cold weather, and they imagine it to be the beams of the house, as the same is heard on board the ship.

N. B. On 14th, at 4 h. I, for the first time, had a fire in the observatory. J. D.
