XXI. Meteorological Observations at Ludgvan in Mount's-Bay, Cornwall, 1769:
By William Bollafe, D.D. F.R.S. Communicated by Dr. Jeremiah Miles, Dean of Exeter, and F.R.S.

Received February 8, 1770.

| Month | Barometer | State of the Weather and Wind | Fahrenheit's Thermom. | Omb. |

| January | Highest 7 30,12 | Calm, cloudy, and howery to the 14th, the ref formy and windy, rain; feet on the 30th; calm and a little roof on the 31st. Wind from all points nearly equal. | Med. Inches. | 46°00' |
| February | Highest 20 30,5 | Rainy and howery, without froth, snow, or hail, to the 8th; on the 9th and 10th half-flowers with fine fleec; high again on the 22nd and 24th; the ref rainy, windy; wind 17 days from the North, the ref mostly from the Wef. | Highest 27 57 | Lowest 21 33 | 43°32' |
| March | Highest 24 30,40 | Showery, not much rain; windy, rather than formy; wind 17 days from the Wef, ref nearly the Wef. | Highest 19 52 | Lowest 31 34 | 46°37' |
| April | Highest 30 30,21 | Showers of flow and hail on the 15th; ditto on the 4th with influence; none on any other day, nor then, any High; calm and fair 12 days, the ref windy; about the middle formy. Wind 17 days North, ref nearly equal. | Highest 29 66 | Lowest 1 37 | 49°50' |
