The
The Barometer made use of for these observations has a tube \( \frac{1}{4} \) of an inch diameter, and holds 2lb of quick-silver.

The height of the quicksilver I generally take about 7 o'clock in the morning, 2 in the afternoon, and 9 at night; and the different changes in the weather as they happened in the day.

I have divided the scale of my barometer in inches, from 28 to 31, and each into 20 parts, so that when you find in this journal 29 \( \frac{1}{2} \), it is 29 inches \( \frac{3}{5} \) parts of an inch.
