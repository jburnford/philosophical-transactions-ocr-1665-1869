Note to be added to page 104, last line, to the word "inquiry."

The following remark on the propositions and demonstrations of Apollonius Pergaeus, equally, or rather more applicable to those of Archimedes, is extracted from Dr. Wallis's Algebra.

"Et quidem meritò censeri posset ille, magnus geometra, et prodigiosæ, tum phantasæ tum memoriae vir, si possibile putemus ut potuerit ille propositiones et demonstrationes perplexas, eo ordine quo ad nos perveniant invenire, absque cujusmodi aliquâ inveniendi arte qualis est quam nos algebraem dicimus."

Dr. Wallis's Algebra, cap. LXXVI.
