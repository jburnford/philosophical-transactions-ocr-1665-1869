Gentlemen, Read Feb. 17, 1761. In a paper I lately laid before you, I endeavoured to demonstrate, that the Cicuta major, which, since the publication of Dr. Storke's work at Vienna, had been used medicinally in England, was the plant intended by that gentleman; and not the Cicuta aquatica, as had been suggested by some practitioners here.
