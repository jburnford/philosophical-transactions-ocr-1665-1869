III. An Observation of the Eclipse of the Moon, by Arthur Dobs', Esq; at Castle-Dobbs near Carrickfergus in Ireland, Feb. the 2d, 1728-9; communicated by Oliver St. John, Esq; F.R.S. Castle-Dobbs, Feb. the 8th, 1728-9. Dear SIR, Having had a fine Observation of the Eclipse of the Moon which happened last Sunday Evening, I have sent you it to communicate to the Royal Society, to ascertain our Longitude here, and in other Places where it was observed. The Observation was made by a nine Foot Glass. Having adjusted a monthly Pendulum Clock by a Meridian Line on the 30th of Jan. and further corrected by the Meridian, Feb. the 6th 1728-9.
