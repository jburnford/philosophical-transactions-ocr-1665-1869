Having, as you will easily perceive, omitted those things that were common or of less Moment, I have set down what seemed most remarkable in the Course of this Operation. Upon which Subject I should not have had so much to say if this elegant Method of rescuing one from imminent Danger, and the most difficult kind of Death, had not been ordinarily described more from Theory and Fancy than from Nature and Practice: And if Surgeons had been accustomed to be half as bold to assist Nature in such an Extremity as both they and many Physicians are sometimes officious to disturb her regular and salutary Steps. Tota illa nocte continenter modice ningebat, coelo tamen sic tenuiter nubilato, ut Lunares maculae saepius utcunque distingui possent; quanquam rarius ac difficilius tempore immersionis: Sub emersionem enim paulatim caelum serenatum fuit, ut circa finem jam penitus innube existeret. Horologium correctum fuit per altitudines Arcturi & aquilae, item ex culminante Spica Virginis ac Lance borea Librae. Diameter Lunae initio eclipsis micrometro dimensa, erat $32'0''$.
