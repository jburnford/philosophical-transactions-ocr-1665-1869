The number of houses rated to the land-tax, as appears by the rates in the council-house, Michaelmas, 1751, As the rates are not always accurately made, and as it is the usual custom not to rate houses, which are untenanted, hospitals, or alms-houses, it will be necessary to make a very large allowance for these deficiencies, especially as many houses are rated in gross under the denomination of several tenements; when they belong to the same landlord in all the several parishes, an allowance of 25 per cent. will be more than sufficient.
