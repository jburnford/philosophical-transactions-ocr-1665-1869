Another child, which I saw, was seized with a dysentery, attended with a very ardent fever; which, notwithstanding my utmost endeavours to relieve it, carried off the poor infant on the third day. Several, almost the whole, of this child's discharges by stool were nothing but blood. Upon opening the body after death, the whole of the intestines were in a very great degree inflamed, and of an intensely deep red colour, and the contents of the abdomen were inexpressibly fetid. Throughout the whole course of the disease, keeping the patient moderately warm and promoting his perspiration, was of great importance; and the not sufficiently attending to this, I more than once saw followed by fatal effects. CVI. Viro inclyto ac de Republica Literaria meritissimo D. C. Morton, Med. Doct. Societati Regiae Scientiarum Londinensi ab Actis S. P. D. Joannes Lulofs. Read Dec. 4, 1762. NISI morbo sic satis diuturno fuisset retentus, dudum, Vir spectatissime, ad te dedisset literas, gratias acturus, quod me quoque illustri societatis Regiae Praesidi ac reliquis ejusdem inembris ita commendare volueris, ut, quantum mihi ex literis quibusdam ex Anglia nislis constitit, inter societatis membra numerari mihi contingat: id omni verum contentione me facturum promitto, ut eo nomine haud indignus penitus haberi queam. Licet autem Patriae Fluvi, quorum cura mihi a summis Imperantibus est commissa, multum istius temporis, quod olim astronomiae ac coelestibus observationibus dare solebam, mihi eripiant, attamen ita Uraniae amor imis medullis est infixus, ut saltem notabiliora coeli phænomena observare nunquam intermittam: ideoque non ingratum fore tibi, Regiaeque Societati confido, si hisce inseram brevem observationum recensionem, quas circa utramque Lunae, ut et circa Solis eclipsin, quae hocce anno contigerunt in nostro observatorio habui.
